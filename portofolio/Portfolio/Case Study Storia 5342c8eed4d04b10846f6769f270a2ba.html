Case Study : Storia

Paint Point

User is a 25 year old private employee who is very burdened with the work he is doing. The high cost of living forces him to work overtime for a meager wage, plus toxic bosses and coworkers make the user unable to set targets and find it difficult to do other things in life and are also prone to mental health issues.

Problem Statement

Users who have overtime jobs at Start-Ups and feel mentally burdened, and need a good work-life balance because they want to improve and improve their quality of life.

Solution Offered

  • Opportunity

    An application feature that can help users conduct counseling with psychologists to overcome their mental health issues.

  • How Might We (HMW)
    1. How do users make an appointment with a psychologist?
    1. How does the application know the complaints felt by the user?
    1. How does the application facilitate the user while in therapy?

Scenario & Task

  • Scenario 1

    Now is a pretty busy day for you. You are now in an office with a high level of stress, overtime demands from your boss are quite solid, and you really need a work-life balance. You also make plans for counseling and make an appointment for counseling with a psychologist.

    1. Completing Log-In
    1. Choose the type of counseling
    1. Determine interaction media and counseling schedule
    1. Choose payment
    1. View the counseling schedule

  • Scenario 2

    After you do counseling with a psychologist, you are asked to do journaling or record everything you want/dream that you want and want to do. This journaling becomes therapy for you, this journaling aims so that you have a target and the courage to limit yourself.

    1. Enter the journaling (dream book) menu
    1. Let’s journaling

Usability Testing

Usability Metric

  • Effectiveness (Easy for users to use features/products to complete tasks/scenarios)
  • Efficiency (how long will it take to complete the task using the prototype)
  • Satisfaction (participant subjectivity when using the product/feature)

System Usability Scale Question

  1. I think I would like to use this tool frequently
  1. I found the tool unnecessarily complex
  1. I thought the tool was easy to use
  1. I think that I would need the support of a technical person to be able to use this system
  1. I found the various functions in this tool were well integrated
  1. I thought there was too much inconsistency in this tool
  1. I would imagine that most people would learn to use this tool very quickly
  1. I found the tool very cumbersome to use
  1. I felt very confident using the tool
  1. I needed to learn a lot of things before I could get going with this tool
